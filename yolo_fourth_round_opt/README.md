Round 4 was developed **after the upgraded Round 3 result**. It was not intended as another small parameter-tuning attempt on the same end-to-end pipeline. Instead, this round explored a **two-stage ROI-based upgrade**: - **Stage 1:** binary localization of tooth / lesion regions - **Stage 2:** lesion-only detection for `Caries`, `Cavity`, and `Crack` The purpose of this round was to test whether a lesion-focused redesign could reduce tooth dominance, improve lesion-category learning, and provide a clearer basis for error diagnosis. --- ## Why Round 4 Was Added The upgraded Round 3 model remained the strongest overall result in the original full-image four-class setting. However, Round 3 also made one structural problem very clear: - the `Tooth` class was much easier and much more dominant than the lesion classes - overall metrics were lifted by the strong `Tooth` performance - minority lesion classes, especially `Crack`, remained unstable - lesion-versus-background confusion was still a major bottleneck Therefore, Round 4 was designed as a **methodological upgrade**, not just another YOLO run. The main idea was to separate the task into: 1. a **binary localization stage** to find tooth / lesion regions 2. a **lesion-only detection stage** to focus on the true downstream lesion problem --- ## Main Round 4 Design ### Stage 1: Binary Localization A lightweight binary segmentation/localization model was trained to identify foreground regions containing tooth / lesion structures. This stage was used to generate ROI proposals for the downstream detector. ### Stage 2: Lesion-Only Detection Using the Stage 1 outputs, a lesion-only dataset was constructed for: - `Caries` - `Cavity` -
